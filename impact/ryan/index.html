Loss of Resilience in Human Mobility Under the Influence of Natural Disasters

Goal of paper: Assess the degree to which the wind speed of a hurricane or typhoon impacts mobility in urban areas (using geotagged social media microblog postings). Our conjecture is that when hurricanes/typhoons reach a certain threshold, the mobility-wind speed curve starts to behave non-linearly (or more non-linearly). In other words, there is a threshold after which mobility begins to be destroyed beyond an ability to bounce back (to use a resilience term).
