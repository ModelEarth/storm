Future papers could...

  1. Capture tweets directly to a NoSQL database with relational query support, such as Spark (an extension of Hadoop).
  2. Use NOAA weather data API or other data service for city wind data. (Online news reporters do not always distinguish between sustained winds and gusts.) Sample using NOAA 3-hour wind data: model.earth/storms
  3. Track 10-minute sustained, 2-minute sustained and gusts using weather data feeds.
  4. Generate city boundaries dynamically using city shape files, and/or use cones for storm paths similar to Yan's study.
  5. Use 3-hour time frames to match storm movement data.
  6. Compare impacts using 3-hour NOAA time periods to distinguish movement before and after strong winds.
  7. Analyse data using shorter time-frames coinciding with peak winds.
  8. Limit origins of travel steps to smaller communities closer to the shore to see if hypothesis 2 is then supported.
  9. Include effect of steps entering and exiting city boundary areas.
  10. Investigate effect of travelers that travel through cities (like Tampa and Jacksonville) and travelers that travel to cities during storms (like San Juan).
  11. Add granular tracking of smaller communities to capture areas closer to coast, but allow for long-steps leaving and entering smaller boundary areas.
  12. A more robust analysis of Tacloban could compare tweet levels across multiple years while documenting how the end of automatic geotagging in 2015 impacted changing geotag levels across multiple cities using data from the Philippines, Japan and Southeast US.
  13. Add more comprehensive mobility data from a cell phone company or popular mobile app.
  14. Establish the approximate home location of individuals (using weekend and evening posts) to include steps when individuals did not tweet from home before traveling to work and other destinations.
  15. Include longer distance travel step segments by including 10 to 50km, 50km to 200km, and over 200 km steps to distingish commutes from regional long step travel. Experiment with different split points (between 40km-100km and 200km-300km) to find points that evenly cluster normal step distances.
  16. Anonymize using zipcodes to provide real-time reporting with demographic details.
  17. Create data exchanges with other university departments, power companies, volunteer organizations, and sustainable development initiatives.
